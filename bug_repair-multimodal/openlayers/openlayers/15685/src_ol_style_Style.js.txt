## Bug Analysis

The issue is related to the `setFill()` method in the `Style` class. According to the issue description, when using a semi-transparent fill color with a VectorTile layer, it creates an unwanted additional semi-transparent layer on top of other layers.

Looking at the code, I can see that the `Style` class has a `setFill()` method that simply sets the `fill_` property. The problem appears to be that when a semi-transparent fill is applied to a VectorTile layer, it's not just filling the vector features but creating an additional overlay layer.

The issue is likely related to how the fill is applied to the entire vector tile rather than just to the geometries within the tile. This is causing the semi-transparent fill to be rendered as an additional layer covering the entire tile area.

## Fix

The solution is to modify how the fill is applied to VectorTile layers. We need to ensure that the fill is only applied to the actual geometries within the tile and not to the entire tile area.
